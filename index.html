01 · The Concept

FIRST LIGHT.
Abib's first moon, met by Bali's first sun.

Abib means "the first month". The brand essence is a return to the natural skin cycle, a quiet reset, a minimalism that earns its calm. Bali in July is the dry season — long mornings, low light, slow days. We took those two ideas and built the campaign on the literal overlap: the moment of first light each day.

Pillar 01 · Visual Language

Golden hour as the brand canvas.

Every piece of content shot in Bali's morning and golden-hour windows. Soft natural light. Skin-first composition. No artificial glow, no studio gloss. Lets Abib's minimalist DNA carry the aesthetic.

Pillar 02 · Product Integration

Morning ritual as the through-line.

The Abib hero lineup naturally maps onto a Bali sunrise routine. Wake up · cleanse with pack-to-foam · eye patch as the coffee · sunstick as the threshold step before the day · lip mask before the pool. Product in context, not on a backdrop.

Pillar 03 · Restraint

Less, in Bali.

No spectacle. No staged influencer-trip chaos. Four creators, three nights, real downtime, real shots. Abib's "less is more" principle applied to the campaign itself — fewer creators, fewer posts, more density per piece.

"The perfection will be completed when there's nothing to take out without the necessity of adding something." — Abib brand statement, en.abib.com/pages/brand

04 · The Output

Six finished pieces of content. Made deliberately.

A small, considered output. No overproduction. Each creator gets one solo short-form to own a hero product. One collab short-form binds the group. One long-form recap carries the trip narrative from start to finish.

4

Solo Short-Form

One short-form video per creator (TikTok + Instagram Reels cross-post). Each one anchored on a different Abib hero product. Creator chooses their own angle within the FIRST LIGHT visual framework — morning ritual, beach moment, sunset wind-down, room reset.

1

Collab Short-Form

One short-form video with all four creators together. Group "moment" content — the Abib package reveal, a sunrise scene, an evening together, or the merch drop unboxing. The piece that signals to followers that a real campaign is happening.

1

Long-Form Trip Film

One long-form recap of the entire trip. YouTube + Reels long edit. Trip narrative arc, behind the scenes, all four creators together, Abib hosting visible in the experience. The piece that becomes the anchor case study and gets reused across Abib's owned channels.

Tarte's #trippinwithtarte trips generate 100M+ impressions per trip. Our scale is intentionally smaller — four creators, six finished pieces — targeting 10M–30M combined impressions across the campaign window at a fraction of the production cost. — Tarte benchmark cited by @nataliebarbu (Dec 2025) · 100M+ impressions per trip
05 · The Package

A personal Abib package. Waiting in the room.

Each creator walks into their room and finds a personalized Abib package — designed for them, tied to the trip. The "First Light" set: curated products in custom packaging, a personal note, a piece of branded merch. The room is lightly styled to match. Captures itself.

The Welcome Package

"First Light Set"

Curated for each creator. Same core, personalized accents. The unboxing IS the first content moment of the trip — built to be filmed naturally on arrival.

  • Both sunsticks (Airy + Quick) — pre-trip skin priming
  • PDRN Volume fill-it Booster crème — the "after sun" reset
  • PDRN lip mask + an eye patch of their choosing
  • Pack-to-foam cleanser — travel-format ritual
  • Custom merch piece (T-shirt or hat from the campaign drop)
  • Hand-written note from the Abib team
The Future Product Hook

"Abib Bali" set, ready for retail.

The package concept doesn't end with the trip. The exact "First Light Set" — minus the personalization — becomes a real Abib product offering on the site afterward. Could be sold as a flat set ("Abib Bali Kit"), or as creator-curated edits ("Abib × Jumy Bali Edit"), or both.

  • Drop alongside trip content (late July / early August)
  • Limited edition framing — ties future-product to creator credibility
  • Pre-built bundle for Olive Young and US Amazon — no additional photography needed beyond the trip output
  • Creator-edition versions extend campaign life across Q3

09 · Why Now

Three reasons this campaign lands in July specifically.

Reason 01

Sunstick season is right now.

Abib's Airy + Quick sunsticks are peak-summer product. Bali is the literal use case. A US K-beauty consumer watching a creator apply Abib sunstick at a Bali beach is buying intent, not just brand awareness.

Reason 02

PDRN cream is mid-launch.

The PDRN Volume fill-it Booster crème is on 4th pre-order — "10K sold in first month." A campaign that introduces this product to US audiences via creator content right now extends that pre-order momentum into full launch.

Reason 03

Content rolls into Q3 windows.

Trip mid-July → posts late July through September. Aligns with Abib's "Prep Your Summer Skin" wave, the just-launched Hyaluronic Boom line, and the natural transition into back-to-routine fall product launches.

10 · Next Steps

From here to a confirmed campaign.

  1. Concept feedback from Abib. Confirm the FIRST LIGHT direction, the product mix, and the trip structure. Adjust anywhere the brand sees a sharper angle.
  2. Budget alignment. Confirm the all-in figure works or scope to a different total. Single number, not line-item negotiation.
  3. Creator #4 sourcing + locking. Sensitive Ones sources and proposes 2–3 female creator candidates for the fourth slot, with media kits and recent work for Abib to choose from.
  4. Discovery call. 30 min with Abib team to lock product mix, packaging direction, custom merch design language, content content angles per creator.
  5. Agreement + start of production. Villa booking, creator contracts, merch into production, brief development. Within 5 business days of the discovery call.
