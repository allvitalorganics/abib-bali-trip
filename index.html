01 · The Concept

Abib = the skincare you bring on summer trips.

Summer travel creates real skin problems. Sun. Heat. Sweat. Pool days. Beach days. Flights. Dry hotel air. Long outdoor hours. This is exactly where Abib can own a stronger consumer memory — not just "clean K-beauty," not just "minimal skincare," but the summer travel skincare routine.

Pillar 01 · Behavior

Two products. One routine.

Before going out: Abib Sunstick. After the sun: Abib PDRN Cream. The campaign is built around one simple, repeatable behavior — the kind of memory consumers can hold without trying.

Pillar 02 · Context

Bali makes the use case true.

People already associate Bali with sun, heat, pool villas, beach days, glowing skin, reset. The setting creates the need — the product is used because the trip demands it. This is the difference between product placement and product context.

Pillar 03 · Restraint

Less, in Bali.

Not a loud party trip. Not a Tarte-style spectacle. Four creators, four nights, real downtime, real shots. Abib's "less is more" principle applied to the campaign itself — quiet, beautiful, intentional, product-led.

"The perfection will be completed when there's nothing to take out without the necessity of adding something." — Abib brand statement, en.abib.com/pages/brand

03 · Visual Language

FIRST LIGHT is how the campaign looks.

Abib means "the first month" — a return to the skin's natural rhythm. Bali gives that idea a physical world: morning light, warm air, water, skin, rest, reset.

Visual · Light

Naturally lit. Always.

Every piece of content shot in Bali's morning and golden-hour windows. Soft natural light, skin-first composition. No artificial glow, no studio gloss.

Visual · Tone

Calm, minimal, warm.

Skin-first. Not overly staged. Not chaotic influencer-trip energy. Abib's version of a brand trip is a quiet one — the content has to feel that way.

Commercial

FIRST LIGHT is the look. Skin Reset is the sell.

The visual language carries Abib's brand DNA. The commercial position — "Abib is the skincare you bring on summer trips" — is what the campaign actually sells.

05 · Content Structure

Six core campaign assets. Built for one memory.

Not just six videos. Six assets built to create one product memory: Abib is the skincare you bring on summer trips. Each creator works around the same two-product routine — day Sunstick, night PDRN Cream — but each gets a different angle.

06 · Commerce Extension

Abib Bali Skin Reset Kit.

The campaign should not end at impressions. The exact routine used during the trip becomes a shoppable product bundle — Abib turns a content moment into a retail moment.

What's in the kit

The exact Bali routine, in a box.

The two hero products, the supporting travel items, and the campaign touch — the same kit the creators used on the trip, productized for the consumer.

  • Core: Abib Sunstick + Abib PDRN Cream
  • Supporting: lip mask · eye patch · cleanser · mask pack or mini
  • Campaign touch: Abib × Bali pouch or box · creator note · limited campaign card
Why it matters

Content expense → retail moment.

The consumer doesn't just watch the creators in Bali. They can buy the exact routine. The kit converts the campaign's content memory into measurable commerce — and gives Abib a clean post-trip launch the campaign content directly markets.

  • Sells alongside the long-form trip film drop
  • Limited campaign card carries the scarcity message
  • CTA: "Shop the routine Daesung, Kai, Jumy, and the SO crew used in Bali"
07 · Villa & Merch

The villa is the campaign set.

Not just a place to sleep. The villa functions as Abib's owned environment for four nights — naturally present in every piece of content, never over-branded.

Villa · Set design

Abib-owned environment.

Sensitive Ones styles the villa with subtle Abib details — present, not loud. The goal is to make Abib feel naturally part of the creators' Bali routine, not to dress the space as an event.

  • Abib welcome kits in each room
  • Abib × Bali skincare station / product display
  • Soft neutral fabric · warm natural light · vanity setup
  • Poolside Sunstick moment · nighttime PDRN reset setup
  • Campaign merch placed naturally — not staged
Merch · Subtle, wearable

Something the creators would actually wear.

Abib × Bali merch should not feel like event staff uniforms. It should feel like something a creator would put on for a real summer day — small mark, soft typography, neutral palette.

  • Cream or off-white T-shirts · minimal typography
  • Small "Abib × Bali" mark · cap or tote optional
  • Travel pouch for the Skin Reset Kit
  • Worn across the trip = Abib visible without forced placement

10 · Why Now

Four reasons the campaign lands in July specifically.

Reason 01

Summer travel is the natural use case.

Abib Sunstick needs a real summer setting. A Bali beach or pool villa makes the product immediately understandable. Audiences don't need to be told why they need it — they see the use case.

Reason 02

PDRN needs a recovery story.

PDRN Cream becomes stronger when attached to a real skin moment: after sun, after heat, after travel, after a long day outside. Bali gives PDRN a reason to exist in the routine.

Reason 03

US K-beauty needs lifestyle context.

Many US consumers know K-beauty products but don't always know when to use them. This campaign gives Abib a clear lifestyle position: the K-beauty skincare brand for summer travel.

A standard sponsored campaign creates awareness. A creator-led brand trip creates memory. When multiple creators show up in the same place, using the same products, inside the same Abib-hosted experience, the campaign feels bigger than a post. It feels like something happened. That is what makes people remember the brand. — Tarte's #trippinwithtarte trips generate 100M+ impressions per trip. Our scale is intentionally smaller — built for memory, not just reach.
